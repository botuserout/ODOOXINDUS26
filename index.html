Rules & Guidelines

Fair play, innovation, and collaboration define the competition.

Rule 01

Team Formation

Teams must consist of a minimum of 1 and a maximum of 4 members. Cross-institution teams are allowed.

Rule 02

Registration

Only the team leader needs to register. Team members must accept the invitation after registration confirmation.

Rule 03

Original Work

All submissions must be developed during the hackathon period. Pre-built or reused projects are not allowed.

Rule 04

Two-Round Evaluation

Round 1 is an online qualification round. Shortlisted teams advance to the offline final round at the campus.

Rule 05

Fair Usage

Any open-source libraries or APIs used must be properly credited.

Rule 06

Code of Conduct

Participants must maintain professional behavior and respect fellow participants, mentors, and organizers.

Rule 07

Submission Deadline

Late submissions will not be evaluated under any circumstances.

Rule 08

Judging Criteria

Projects will be evaluated based on innovation, feasibility, technical implementation, and presentation quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before participating.

1. What is the format of the hackathon?

The hackathon consists of two rounds. The first round is an online qualification challenge where teams solve a problem remotely. Shortlisted teams advance to the second round, which is an offline final round conducted at the campus.

2. How many members can be in a team?

Each team must have a minimum of 1 member and a maximum of 4 members.

3. Is registration free?

Yes, registration is completely free and mandatory for participation.

4. Who needs to register for the team?

Only the team leader needs to complete the registration. Team members will receive an invitation to join the team after registration.

5. What happens in the online qualification round?

Teams will receive a surprise problem statement and will have a fixed time window to submit their solution. Only the team leader can submit the final response.

6. How are teams selected for the offline final round?

Top-performing teams from the online round will be shortlisted based on evaluation criteria such as innovation, feasibility, and problem-solving approach.

7. Do participants need to bring their own laptops for the offline round?

Yes, all shortlisted teams must bring their own laptops and required tools for the final round.

8. Will accommodation or travel expenses be provided?

No travel or accommodation allowances will be provided for this event.

9. Can students from different institutions form a team?

Yes, cross-institution teams are allowed and encouraged.

10. How will important updates be communicated?

All official updates will be communicated via email to the team leader.
